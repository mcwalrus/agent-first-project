# Final Notes

This is a summary of my thoughts on the topic of AI.

## Implications

While I, like many software engineers, am directly affected by these developments, the implications stretch far beyond our industry. AI is disrupting all knowledge-based work professions simultaneously - and anyone willing to adapt stands to benefit, at least for now. Our current economy depends on human labour, by means of paying income-based tax to the state, while keeping people off social support services.

AI has rapidly found it's way into almost every sector of business within the western ecconomy. If agents displace that labour at scale, the question of what's left for the rest of us will become unavoidable. The pressure is already here for most student graduates, regular people, as well as skilled labour individuals who are outside the labour market. For software engineers, the role is shifting dramatically - that is, using AI to automate not just our own work, but everyone else's too.

My goal of this tutorial is to make people self-aware of our new economic reality. I believe what is happening deserves serious political conversation. Almost no-one has noticed the cost of AI-driven software development fall below the cost of minimum wage. AI is a taboo subject which no-one openly wants to be caught using unless it is championed by. No human worker, no matter how skilled, or how willing to adapt can compete with that on price.

Previous technological disruptions displaced certain kinds of work, but they also created new roles, new industries, new demand for human effort. This time things are fundamentally different. With AI, we have a technology capable of making autonomous decisions itself. We have never had a previous technology work like this before. And if you are using the right models you'll know really good. I already know because I use it everyday. I expect AI is currently being applied to tests of being personal assistants, business managers, or effectively operating entire companies end-to-end if achitected well.

## Risks

Greater nations may understand that those who control the governance and access to AI models control means to power. AI model compute is fundimentally equivellent to intellegent slave labour. This is why we are seeing a huge majority of datacenters being built during a time people consider an 'AI bubble'. What it is, is actually not an 'AI bubble', but 'A bubble caused by AI'. AI is well worth what it is, and currently we pay far cheaper than what we should for it to subside training of the models. I fear this will cause the desperate ecconomic conditions across nations that will either force or lead us down the path towards a state of war.

Without credible intervention for smaller western ecconomies, we may quietly see the erosion and disappearance of our workforce. One that pays back to our society through it's income dependent supporters and tax-base. How would ecconomies look to support themselves if everyone was to properly understand this? Are nations even aware of this trojan horse which has entered their economies. I largely suspect not.

Ultimately, I believe there is a fundamental change shifting in what the social contract of money means. That is, the idea of money representing "work done". If you work for me, then I pay you, and I hope in return I can hopefully run, maintain or grow my business. What happens when we all start going to the model providers out of a shared awareness in our pursuit of growth? Will this contract disolve? It's an open question I will continue to ask until I gain a proper answer for it.

Will we no longer rely on money if the majority of our skilled labour is happening through machines? How will countries look to adapt to tax businesses that are operating without their own compute? Lots of questions with no political answers yet. At the moment, following the current ecconomic conditions, we might be in for an ecconomic course we cannot recover from. If I am wrong for asking, please tell me what we are going to do that is right.
