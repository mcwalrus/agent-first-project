## Implications

While I, like many software engineers, am directly affected by these developments, the implications stretch far beyond our industry. AI is disrupting all knowledge-based work simultaneously — and anyone willing to adapt stands to benefit, at least for now. But that window feels uncertain when our economy depends on human labour across every sector. If agents displace that labour at scale, the question of what's left for the rest of us becomes unavoidable. For software engineers, the role has quietly shifted into something strange: using AI to automate not just our own work, but everyone else's too.

My goal in presenting this is to make people aware. I believe what is happening deserves serious political conversation. Almost no-one has noticed the cost of AI-driven software development already operates below minimum wage in cost. No human worker no matter how skilled or how willing to adapt can compete with that on price. Previous technological disruptions displaced certain kinds of work, but they also created new roles, new industries, new demand for human effort. This time things are fundamentally different. With AI, we have a technology capable of making autonomous decisions itself. One that can act as a personal assistant, a business manager, or effectively an entire company operating end-to-end if achitected well.

## Risks

I hold this as an existential concern. Nations that realise this might understand that for those who control governance and access to the models will hold all power. Without credible intervention for smaller ecconomies, we may quietly see the erosion and disappearance of our workforce. One that pays back to our society through it's current income dependent tax-base. How would ecconomies look to support themselves if everyone was to properly understand this? Are nations even aware of this trojan horse which has entered their economies. I really suspect not.

Ultimately, I believe there is a fundamental change shifting in what the social contract of money means. That is, the idea of money representing "work done". If you work for me, then I pay you, and I hope in return I can hopefully run, maintain or grow my business. What happens when we all start going to the model providers out of a shared awareness in our pursuit of growth? Will this contract disolve? It's an open question I will continue to ask until I gain a proper answer for it.

Will we no longer rely on money if the majority of our skilled labour is happening through machines? How will countries look to adapt to tax businesses that are operating without their own compute? Lots of questions with no political answers yet. At the moment, following the current ecconomic conditions, we might be in for an ecconomic course we cannot recover from. If I am wrong for asking, please tell me what we are going to do that is right.
